Good News Club

Bringing the Gospel to children in public elementary schools across Palm Beach and Martin Counties.

After-School Ministry

What Is Good News Club?

Good News Club is a ministry of Child Evangelism Fellowship in which trained teachers meet with groups of children in schools, homes, community centers, churches, and apartment complexes—anywhere children can easily and safely meet with their parent's permission.

Each week the teacher presents an exciting Bible lesson using colorful materials from CEF Press. This action-packed time also includes songs, Scripture memory, a missions story, and review games focused on the lesson's theme.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes! In 2001, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled in Good News Club v. Milford Central School that Good News Clubs can meet in public schools after school hours on the same terms as other community groups. Children attend only with their parents' permission.

Each week an exciting Bible lesson is presented along with songs, Scripture memory, a missions story, and review games. Small Groups and Wonder Time help children dive deeper into their relationship with God. Every club includes a clear presentation of the Gospel and strong discipleship training. All children are encouraged to attend a local church.

Christians who are concerned for boys and girls volunteer their time and energy. They are trained by CEF to lead clubs that are exciting and informative. Teachers sign the CEF Statement of Faith and agree to abide by organizational policies. All adults are screened according to our child protection policy.

Good News Club is completely free for all children to attend.
