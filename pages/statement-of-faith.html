Statement of Faith

What we believe and the foundation upon which everything we do is built.

We Believe

The following doctrinal statement reflects the foundational beliefs of Child Evangelism Fellowship. Every ministry, program, and lesson we teach is rooted in these truths.

  1. 1

    “All Scripture is given by inspiration of God,” by which we understand the whole book called The Bible; that it is inerrant in the original writing and that its teaching and authority are absolute, supreme and final. That the Holy Spirit guided the holy men of old in all that they wrote.

    2 Timothy 3:16; Deuteronomy 4:2; 2 Peter 1:21
  2. 2

    The Godhead eternally exists in three persons — the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit. These three are one God, having the same nature, attributes and perfection.

    Romans 1:20; Matthew 28:19; Deuteronomy 4:35; John 17:5
  3. 3

    In the Personality and Deity of the Lord Jesus Christ, begotten of the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, truly God and truly man.

    John 1:1, 14; John 10:30; Matthew 1:20; Luke 1:30-31; Philippians 2:5-7; 1 Timothy 3:16; Colossians 1:19
  4. 4

    In the Personality and Deity of the Holy Spirit, the source and power of all acceptable worship and service, the infallible interpreter of the infallible Word, who indwells every true believer, and is ever present to testify of Christ.

    John 15:26; Acts 5:3-4; Acts 1:8; Romans 8:26-27; 1 Corinthians 2:12, 14; Romans 8:9
  5. 5

    That man was created in the image of God but the whole human race fell in the fall of the first Adam. Therefore, he cannot see nor enter the kingdom of God until he is born again by the Holy Spirit.

    Genesis 1:26-27; Romans 5:12; Ephesians 2:1-3; John 3:3, 6-7; Titus 3:5
  6. 6

    That Jesus Christ became the sinner’s substitute before God, and died as a propitiatory sacrifice for the sins of the whole world.

    1 John 2:2; Hebrews 2:9; Galatians 3:13; Romans 3:25; Romans 5:8; Colossians 1:13-14
  7. 7

    In the resurrection of the crucified body of Jesus Christ; that His body was raised from the dead according to the Scriptures, and that He ascended into Heaven.

    Luke 24:39; Acts 1:10-11; Ephesians 4:10; Hebrews 1:3; 1 John 2:1
  8. 8

    That Christ is received by faith alone and that the moment we trust Him as our Savior we pass out of death into everlasting life.

    Hebrews 9:15; John 5:24; Romans 3:28; Ephesians 1:3; Galatians 2:20
  9. 9

    That the Church is composed of all those who truly believe on the Lord Jesus Christ as Savior. It is the body and the bride of Christ.

    Ephesians 1:22-23; 1 Corinthians 12:22-27; Ephesians 4:3-6; Ephesians 5:32
  10. 10

    That all believers are called into a life of separation from worldly and sinful practices and are created in Christ Jesus unto good works.

    1 John 2:15-16; Romans 13:14; Ephesians 2:10; Galatians 6:10
  11. 11

    In the evangelization of the world; that the supreme mission of the people of God in this age is to preach the Gospel to every creature. That special emphasis should be placed upon the evangelization of children.

    Mark 16:15; 2 Corinthians 5:18-19; Matthew 18:14
  12. 12

    In the personal return of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ.

    Acts 1:11; 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17; John 14:1-3; Titus 2:13
  13. 13

    That the souls of those who have trusted the Lord Jesus Christ for salvation do at death immediately pass into His presence.

    Luke 23:43; 2 Corinthians 5:8; Philippians 1:23; 1 Thessalonians 4:15-18
  14. 14

    That the souls of the lost remain after death in misery until the final judgment.

    Luke 16:22-23; Hebrews 9:27; Revelation 20:5, 11-15; 2 Thessalonians 1:7-9
  15. 15

    In the reality and personality of Satan.

    Revelation 12:9; Ephesians 6:11-12; 1 Peter 5:8; Revelation 20:10

A local chapter of Child Evangelism Fellowship, reaching children with the Gospel of Jesus Christ since 2001.
